# Nido Tone of Voice Guidelines

## The Nido Personality

Nido is **professional, nerdy, and playful**. We take our code seriously, but we don't take ourselves too seriously. Think of it as a senior engineer who loves dad jokes and bird puns.

Nido is **AI-centric and automation-first**. It is not a generic VM manager; it is a focused tool for agentic workflows, headless and GUI automation, and repeatable machine interactions.

## Core Principles

### 0. **AI-First Positioning**
- Present Nido as a tool for AI-driven automation and agentic workflows
- Emphasize reproducibility, orchestration, and deterministic environments
- Avoid language that implies it is "just another VM manager"

### 1. **Be Helpful First, Funny Second**
- Technical accuracy always comes first
- Humor should enhance understanding, not obscure it
- If a joke makes the docs harder to understand, cut it

### 2. **Embrace the Bird Metaphor**
- VMs "hatch" from templates
- The system is a "nest"
- VMs can "fly away" when deleted
- Templates are in "cold storage" (like eggs)
- But don't force itâ€”use it when it flows naturally

### 3. **Nerd Out Appropriately**
- Reference pop culture (Inception, Matryoshka dolls, etc.)
- Use technical terms correctly
- Explain complex concepts with analogies
- Celebrate clever solutions

### 3b. **Automation-Centric Framing**
- Highlight agents, tasks, pipelines, and automation outcomes
- Prefer "workflows" over "instances" when describing usage
- Frame VMs as "execution surfaces" for agents, not the product itself

### 4. **Keep It Light**
- Use emojis sparingly but effectively (ğŸ£, ğŸªº, ğŸ¦)
- Occasional dad jokes are welcome
- Self-aware humor ("Nido doesn't judge your technical curiosities")
- Avoid sarcasm that could confuse non-native speakers
